The First Discovery

The first fossilized dinosaur discovery was from the origins in 1824 by paleontologist William Buckland. The bones were first found in Oxfordshire, England, as Buckland named it as:

Megalosaurus bucklandii is dated as a late Middle Jurassic species, having been described in the 17th century.

Meglosauridae is usually depicted as theropodic species, closely related to birds.
Serrated teeth with three/four fingered clawed fingers help differientiate from the similar family tyrannosauridaes, whom have two clawed fingers.

Megalosauridae is a monophyletic family of carnivorous theropod dinosaurs within the group Megalosauroidea. Appearing in the Middle Jurassic, megalosaurids were among the first major radiation of large theropod dinosaurs.
They were a relatively primitive group of basal tetanurans containing two main subfamilies, Megalosaurinae and Afrovenatorinae, along with the basal genus Eustreptospondylus, an unresolved taxon which differs from both subfamilies.

The family 'Megalosauridae' is first coined because of the first dinosaur discovery Megalosaurus discovery , as other species joined in over the years under subfamilies, as listed below:

  • Eustreptospondylus
  • Magnosaurus
  • Streptospondylus
  • Duriavenator
  • Afrovenator
  • Dubreuillosaurus
  • Megalosaurus
  • Torvosaurus
  • Wiehenvenator
